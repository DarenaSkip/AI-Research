Idea:
Recent-apps view and floating AI presence could evolve into a no-window interaction model.

Core thought:
If an AI becomes recognizable enough to the user, a full app window is not always necessary.
A small persistent presence, shortcut, or contextual layer could be enough.

Two UX directions:
1. Android-style recent apps / task-switcher insight:
   - Why always require a full visible window?
   - If the user already knows what the AI is and what it does, reopening a full interface may be unnecessary friction.

2. Floating / live AI layer:
   - A small always-available presence on screen could be more natural than opening a full app.
   - The AI would feel less like a separate tool and more like a contextual layer.

Main UX principle:
Recognition can reduce interface weight.

In simple words:
The more familiar and identifiable the AI becomes,
the less traditional UI it needs.

Possible future concept:
AI as a lightweight presence, not a heavy app window.

One-line version:
If recognition is strong enough, the window becomes optional.
